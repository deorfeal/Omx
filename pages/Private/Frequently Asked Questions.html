Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are proxies and how do they work?

Proxies are intermediaries between your device and the internet. When you connect to a proxy server, your web traffic is routed through the proxy, masking your real IP address and providing you with a different IP. This helps with anonymity, security, and access to geo-blocked content.

2. How do I set up a proxy on my device?

Setting up a proxy depends on your device and the platform you’re using. In most cases, you can configure proxies in your browser settings or at the system level by navigating to your network settings and entering the proxy details provided by OmexAI. We also provide detailed setup guides for various devices.

3. What’s the difference between static and dynamic proxies?

Static proxies provide a fixed IP address that remains constant throughout your session. This is useful for tasks that require a stable connection. Dynamic proxies, on the other hand, change your IP address regularly, making it harder to track your activity and ideal for situations requiring a high level of anonymity.

4. How do I choose between residential, mobile, and data center proxies?

Residential proxies use IPs from real homes, offering the highest level of anonymity and authenticity. Mobile proxies use IPs assigned by mobile carriers, making them perfect for mobile traffic simulations. Data center proxies are faster and more affordable, but they may be detected more easily. Choose based on your specific needs.

6. How do I switch between different proxy servers?

Switching between proxy servers is simple. If you're using a proxy management tool or browser extension, you can easily change the proxy IP within the tool. Alternatively, you can update the proxy settings manually by entering new proxy credentials in your device or browser settings.

7. What is time-based bandwidth and how does it work?

Time-based bandwidth charges you based on the amount of time you use the proxy service rather than the volume of data you transfer. This model is perfect for users who need temporary or short-term access without worrying about exceeding data limits.
